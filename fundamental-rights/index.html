Fundamental Rights of American Democracy

Dignity of the Individual

What does it mean?

Dignity of the Individual means that everyone has inherent and inalienable value.

Where does it come from?

It comes from the philosophical, humanistic, or religious belief that everyone has equal value regardless of social status.

What does it look like?

It looks like equal treatment under the law and equal access to resources that allow for a decent life.

Equality

What does it mean?

Equality is the concept that all individuals have the same rights and should therefore be treated equally.

Where does it come from?

It comes from the Declaration of Independence and the belief that all people share the same natural rights.

What does it look like?

It looks like equal protection under the law and fair access to opportunities without discrimination.

Citizenship

What does it mean?

Citizenship means being a legal member of a nation with specific rights, responsibilities, and participation in civic life.

Where does it come from?

It comes from constitutional law, national identity, and the social contract between people and government.

What does it look like?

It involves voting, obeying laws, serving on juries, and contributing to the community.

Pathways to Citizenship for Noncitizens

What does it mean?

This is the legal processes that allows immigrants and noncitizens to legally become full members of our country.

Where does it come from?

It comes from immigration laws, constitutional principles, and national policies defining who can join the country.

What does it look like?

It looks like naturalization, green card programs, asylum and refugee processes, and other legal immigration pathways.

Majority Rule

What does it mean?

Majority rule means that decisions are made based on what most people or representatives choose.

Where does it come from?

It comes from the democratic principle that power comes from the will of the people.

What does it look like?

It looks like elections where the highest number of votes win and policies that follow the preferences of the majority of citizens.

Minority Rights

What does it mean?

Minority rights mean that people who are not in the majority are still protected and cannot have their rights taken away.

Where does it come from?

It comes from constitutional protections, especially the Bill of Rights, which ensure that basic rights cannot be overridden by the majority.

What does it look like?

It looks like protections against discrimination for unpopular opinions, minority groups, and other individuals.

Compromise

What does it mean?

Compromise means finding a middle ground so that different groups can agree on a solution.

Where does it come from?

It comes from the practical need for cooperation in a democracy where people hold different views.

What does it look like?

It looks like laws created through negotiation and policies that blend competing interests together.

Individual Freedom

What does it mean?

Individual freedom means that people can make their own choices as long as they do not harm others.

Where does it come from?

Individual freedoms come mainly from the constitution, such as the first amendment.

What does it look like?

It looks like freedom of speech, religion, and other personal decision-making.
